PART 1 Instructions

Page 1 out of 2

 

In Part 1, you will be presented with 100 sliders. Your task is to position as many of the 100 sliders at the center of the respective scale as possible within 5 minutes.

Initially, each slider is at the very left end of the scale (position 0) and can be moved on the scale with the help of the mouse, maximally to the very right end of the scale (position 100). To move the slider, you have to press the left mouse button. The arrows on the keyboard and the mouse wheel are deactivated. A slider will count as correctly positioned at the center if you have positioned it between positions {{correct_lower_bound}} and {{correct_upper_bound}} (so either exactly at the middle position 50 or very nearby position 50).

The below is an exemplar slider. Try to move the slider and get the sense of where the middle position is. Unlike this practice slider, the current position of each slider in the actual task will not be shown. You will have to estimate on your own where the middle of the scale is.




At the end of Part 1, you will receive a total of 100 balls, each of which is Green or Black.

  • For every correctly positioned slider, you will receive one Green ball.
  • For every incorrectly positioned slider, you will receive one Black ball.

Notice that the number of Green balls you will have after Part 1 equals the number of sliders you positioned correctly.
You will not learn how many Green balls you received until the end of the experiment.

 

PART 1 Instructions

Page 2 out of 2

 

For every Green ball you received, you earn {{point_per_correct_slider}} Experimental Dollar. For every black ball you received, you earn nothing. Therefore, you could earn more Experimental Dollars as you position more sliders correctly, thereby receiving more Green balls. The number of Green balls you have after Part 1 also plays an important role in your payoff from the later parts of the experiment.

The remaining time will be shown on the top of the screen. When 5 minutes of the working time is over, the number of sliders you positioned correctly will be counted and saved by the computer. Then, you will be presented with Part 2 automatically.
