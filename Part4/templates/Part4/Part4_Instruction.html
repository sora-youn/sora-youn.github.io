PART 4 Instructions

Page 1 out of 4

 

In Part 4 of the experiment, you are asked to revise two estimates that you provided in Part 2:

1. Your estimate concerning the probability that you received a certain number of red balls from Part 1

2. Your estimate concerning the probability that your teammate received a certain number of red balls from Part 1.


Reminder:

The numbers of red balls you and your teammate received in Part 1 equal to the numbers of sliders you and your teammate correctly positioned, out of 100 sliders, respectively.


By providing each of the estimates, you have the possibility to earn 30 points. The more precise your estimate is, the more likely it is that you will earn the 30 points. Since you are asked to provide two estimates for you and for your teammate, you will earn 60 points to the maximum from Part 4.

 

PART 4 Instructions

Page 2 out of 4

 

To provide each of the estimates, you are given 100 tokens and asked to distribute them into 10 pillars. Each token represents one percentage point. 

Specifically, the screen which we use for asking for your estimates looks like this:

 

  • There are 10 pillars. Each pillar represents a certain quantity (in steps of 10) of red balls you (your teammate) received in Part 1.
  • To provide an estimate concerning the probability that you received a certain number of red balls, you are asked to distribute 100 tokens into the 10 pillars in “# of Red balls YOU received” column. You can distribute tokens to a pillar by typing an integer, ranging from 0 to 100.
  • Similarly, to provide an estimate concerning the probability that your teammate received a certain number of red balls, type an integer, ranging from 0 to 100, in each pillar of  “# of Red balls YOUR TEAMMATE received” column. 
  • The more likely you deem a certain pillar to contain the number of red balls you (your teammate) received, the more tokens should be put into this pillar.
  • Whenever you type an integer in any pillar, the last row of the decision table "# of tokens you have distributed so far" will be updated.
  • The task will only be finished when you have distributed exactly 100 tokens into the 10 pillars in each of “# of Red balls YOU received” and “# of Red balls YOUR TEAMMATE received” columns.
  • If you feel confident about the resulting probability distributions because they are good fits to your estimates concerning the quantity of red balls you and your teammate received, please press the “Confirm” button downright to continue with Part 5 of the experiment.

 

PART 4 Instructions

Page 3 out of 4

 

Here is an example of how it works. Suppose you have distributed tokens in the following way.

  • First, look at “# of Red balls YOU received” column. You have placed 50 tokens in each of “11-20” pillar and “21-30” pillar. This implies that you assume that with 50% probability you received between 11 and 20 red balls and with 50% probability you received between 21 and 30 red balls.
  • Now, look at “# of Red balls YOUR TEAMMATE received” column. You have placed 77 tokens in “71-80” pillar and 23 tokens in “81-90”. This implies that with 77% probability you assume that your teammate received between 71 and 80 red balls and with 23% probability you assume that your teammate received between 81 and 90 red balls.
