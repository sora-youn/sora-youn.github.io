Whenever a ball is drawn from whichever urn, the drawn ball is replaced with the same-colored ball. Therefore, the probabilities of drawing a red ball (and the probabilities of drawing a blue ball) from Urn 1 and Urn 2 do NOT change as you keep drawing until 100 balls are drawn.


For example, suppose out of 100 balls in Urn 1, 50 balls are red. Further, assume that 70 balls are red, out of 100 balls in Urn 2. This implies:

  • Whenever a ball is drawn from Urn 1, the ball is red with probability 50% and blue with probability 50%.
  • Whenever a ball is drawn from Urn 2, the ball is red with probability 70% and blue with probability 30%.
  • The probability of drawing a red (blue) ball from each urn remains constant during the entire experiment.

How do you earn points?

Once you submit your decision on how many balls to draw from each urn, the computer draws 100 balls accordingly.
Then, you receive lottery tickets depending on the colors of the drawn balls.

  • For every drawn RED ball, you are awarded 10 lottery tickets or zero lottery tickets.
  • For every drawn BLUE ball, you receive zero lottery tickets.

The computer draws a random number "X" from the interval [0,1000]. Any number within the interval [0,1000] is equally likely to be selected. The number of lottery tickets you received and the random number "X" determines your earning from Part 3.

  • If "X" is less than or equal to the total number of lottery tickets awarded to you, you earn 200 points.
  • If "X" is greater than the total number of lottery tickets awarded to you, you earn zero points.

At the end of Part 3, i.e. when you finished all of {{num_game_round}} rounds, one of the earnings from these {{num_game_round}} rounds will be randomly chosen as your payment from Part 3.


IMPORTANT:

  • In every round, you have the possibility to earn 200 points.
  • For every drawn RED ball, you have a chance to receive 10 lottery tickets.
  • The more lottery tickets you are awarded, the more likely you earn 200 points.
  • Every 10 lottery tickets awarded to you increases the probability of earning 200 points by 1%.

How do I maximize the probability of earning 200 points?

To assist your decision-making, we provide you a decision tool.

On the top of the decision-making screen, you will be asked to enter your guesses about the numbers of red balls that you and your teammate received from Part 1.

Conditional on the entered guesses, the software calculates the optimal decision for you.
By "optimal decision", we mean a decision that, conditional on your guesses, will maximize the expected number of lottery tickets that will be awarded to you, and thus will maximize the probability of you winning 200 points. The software also calculates, conditional on your guesses, the number of lottery tickets that you are expected to receive when you follow the recommendation.

You may enter different guesses as many times as you wish. Whenever you enter new guesses, the software will provide a new optimal decision accordingly.

    Probability that a drawn ball awards you 10 lottery tickets


    Every drawn ball awards you 10 lottery tickets with the following probability:

    Pr(a drawn ball is RED | Urn "i") X ( # of balls drawn from Urn "i" )-.5

     

    1. The first term implies that you have a chance to receive 10 lottery tickets only when the drawn ball is RED. Therefore, the more red balls you draw, the more likely you receive more lottery tickets, and thus the more likely you earn 200 points. This means that you should draw more balls from Urn 1 if you believe Urn 1 contains more red balls than Urn 2. On the contrary, if you believe Urn 2 contains more red balls than Urn 1, you should draw more balls from Urn 2.


    2. The second term implies that, given that a ball drawn from Urn "i" is red, the probability of the drawn red ball awarding you 10 lottery tickets is exponentially decreasing as you draw more balls from Urn "i". The below figure visualizes the relationship. The x-axis represents the number of balls that you decided to draw from Urn "i". The y-axis represents the probability that a drawn red ball from Urn "i" awards you 10 lottery tickets. The figure implies:

    • As you decided to draw more balls from a single urn (Urn "i"), the probability that a red ball drawn from that urn gives you 10 lottery tickets gets lower.
    • Each additional ball you decided to draw from a single urn (Urn "i") lowers the probability that a red ball drawn from that urn gives you 10 lottery tickets less than the previous ball lowered the probability.
