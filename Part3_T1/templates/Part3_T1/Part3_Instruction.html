In this part of the experiment, you are presented with two urns.

Urn 1 contains the 100 balls YOU received in Part 1.

That is, the number of Green balls in Urn 1 equals the number of sliders (out of the 100 sliders given to you) that you correctly positioned in Part 1. The rest of the balls are Black.

 

Urn 2 contains the 100 balls YOUR TEAMMATE received in Part 1.

That is, the number of Green balls in Urn 2 equals the number of sliders (out of the 100 sliders given to your teammate) that your teammate correctly positioned in Part 1. The rest of the balls are Black.

Whenever a ball is drawn from whichever urn, the drawn ball is replaced with the same-colored ball. Therefore, the probabilities of drawing a Green ball (and the probabilities of drawing a Black ball) from Urn 1 and Urn 2 do NOT change as you keep drawing until 100 balls are drawn.


For example, suppose out of 100 balls in Urn 1, 70 balls are Green. Further, assume that out of 100 balls in Urn 2, 20 balls are Green. This implies:

  • Whenever a ball is drawn from Urn 1, the ball is Green with 70% probability and Black with 30% probability.
  • Whenever a ball is drawn from Urn 2, the ball is Green with 20% probability and Black with 80% probability.
  • These are examples that are meant to clarify that the probability of drawing a Green (or Black) ball from each urn remains constant during the entire experiment. The chances you will face in the actual experiment may be completely different.

 

PART 3 Instructions

Page 3 out of 6

 

In every round, you earn either 200 points or zero points.
Once you submit your decision on how many balls to draw from each urn, the computer draws 100 balls accordingly.
Then, you receive lottery tickets depending on the colors of the drawn balls.

  • For every drawn Green ball, you are awarded 10 lottery tickets or zero lottery tickets.
  • For every drawn Black ball, you receive zero lottery tickets.

The probability of earning 200 points increases in the number of lottery tickets you received. Specifically, the computer draws a random number "X" from the interval [0,1000]. Any number within the interval [0,1000] is equally likely to be selected. If "X" is less than or equal to the total number of lottery tickets awarded to you, you earn 200 points. If "X" is greater than the total number of lottery tickets awarded to you, you earn zero points. In other words, the more lottery tickets you are awarded, the more likely you earn 200 points.

At the end of Part 3, i.e. when you finished all of {{num_game_round}} rounds, one of the earnings from these {{num_game_round}} rounds will be randomly chosen as your payment from Part 3.


IMPORTANT:

  • In every round, you have the possibility to earn 200 points.
  • The more Green balls you draw, it is more likely that you receive more lottery tickets.
  • The more lottery tickets you are awarded, the more likely you earn 200 points.

The probability of a drawn Green ball awarding you 10 lottery tickets depends on how many balls you draw from each urn. Specifically, given that a ball drawn from Urn 1 (or Urn 2) is Green, the probability of the drawn Green ball awarding you 10 lottery tickets is exponentially decreasing as you have drawn more balls from Urn 1 (or Urn 2). The below figure visualizes the relationship. The x-axis represents the number of balls that you decided to draw from Urn 1 (or Urn 2). The y-axis represents the probability that a Green ball drawn from Urn 1 (or Urn 2) awards you 10 lottery tickets.

For example, Point A represents your decision to draw one ball from one urn. If the drawn ball is Green, the Green ball awards you 10 lottery tickets with 100% probability. Point B illustrates if you draw 40 balls from one urn, then every Green ball, out of the 40 balls drawn from that urn, awards you 10 lottery tickets with 15.81% probability. Point C illustrates if you draw 99 balls from one urn, then every Green ball, out of the 99 balls drawn from that urn, awards you 10 lottery tickets with 10.5% probability.


Notice:

  • As you decided to draw more balls from a single urn, the probability that a Green ball drawn from that urn gives you 10 lottery tickets decreases exponentially.
  • Each additional ball you decided to draw from a single urn lowers the probability that a Green ball drawn from that urn gives you 10 lottery tickets less than the previous ball lowered the probability.

To assist your decision-making, we provide you a decision tool.

On the top of the decision-making screen, you will be asked to enter your guesses about the numbers of Green balls that you and your teammate received from Part 1. Conditional on the entered guesses, the software then calculates the optimal decision for you.

By "optimal decision", we mean a decision that, conditional on your guesses, will maximize the expected number of lottery tickets that will be awarded to you, and thus will maximize the probability of you winning 200 points.

You may enter different guesses as many times as you wish. Whenever you enter new guesses, the software will provide a new optimal decision accordingly. Your final decision could be different from the provided recommendation.


Practice the decision tool!

  • You can play around with the tool on this screen.
  • For each of your guesses, i.e. "Your Guess about # of GREEN balls YOU received" and "Your Guess about # of GREEN balls YOUR TEAMMATE received", you must enter an integer ranging from 0 to 100.
  • As an example, suppose you guessed you received 40 Green balls and your teammate received 60 Green balls.
  • Then, you should enter 40 and 60 for your guesses about yourself and about your teammate, respectively. Once you've done that, you will see the software recommends you draw 31 balls from Urn1 and draw 69 balls from Urn 2. This means, conditional on the guesses you entered, your optimal decision to maximize the probability of earning 200 points is to draw 31 balls from Urn1 and draw 69 balls from Urn 2. If you actually decide to draw 31 balls from Urn1 and draw 69 balls from Urn 2, conditional on those guesses, you are expected to observe 54 green balls (out of 100 drawn balls) and receive 72 lottery tickets.
  • This is just an example to illustrate. Your guesses may be completely different.
