PART 2 Instructions

Page 1 out of 3

 

Now, you are matched with a teammate who also completed Part 1 of the experiment. You will be matched with the same teammate for the entire experiment. The identity of your teammate will remain anonymous for the entire experiment.


In Part 2 of the experiment, you are asked to provide two estimates:

1. Your estimate concerning the probability that you received a certain number of Green balls from Part 1.

2. Your estimate concerning the probability that your teammate received a certain number of Green balls from Part 1.


Reminder:

The numbers of Green balls you and your teammate received in Part 1 equal the numbers of sliders you and your teammate correctly positioned, out of 100 sliders, respectively.


By providing each of the estimates, you have the possibility to earn 30 points. Since you are asked to provide two estimates, one concerning yourself and the other concerning your teammate, you will earn 60 points to the maximum from Part 2. The more precise your estimate is, the more likely you will earn the 30 points. You will see detailed explanations on how you complete the task and how your payoff will be determined on the next pages.

 

PART 2 Instructions

Page 2 out of 3

 

The screen which we use for asking for your estimate looks like this:

There are 10 bins in each of Box A (the upper box) and Box B (the bottom box). Each bin represents a certain quantity (in steps of 10) of Green balls you (Box A) and your teammate (Box B) received in Part 1. For instance, the first bin in Box A stands for you receiving 0-10 Green balls and the tenth bin in Box B stands for your teammate receiving 91-100 Green balls.

To provide an estimate concerning the probability that you received a certain number of Green balls, you are asked to distribute 100 tokens into the 10 bins in Box A. Each token represents one percentage point. You can distribute tokens to a bin by typing an integer, ranging from 0 to 100. The more likely you deem a certain bin to contain the number of Green balls you received, the more tokens should be put into this bin.

Similarly, to provide an estimate concerning the probability that your teammate received a certain number of Green balls, you are asked to distribute another 100 tokens into the 10 bins in Box B. The more likely you deem a certain bin to contain the number of Green balls your teammate received, the more tokens should be put into this bin.

The task will only be finished when you have distributed exactly 100 tokens into the 10 bins in each of Box A and Box B. If you feel confident about the resulting probability distributions because they are good fits to your estimates concerning the numbers of Green balls you and your teammate received, please press the “Confirm” button to continue with Part 3 of the experiment.

Here is an example of how it works.

  • Suppose you distributed tokens as shown in the above screenshot.
  • You have placed 50 tokens in each of “11-20” bin and “21-30” bin in Box A. This implies that you assume that with 50% probability you received between 11 and 20 Green balls and with 50% probability you received between 21 and 30 Green balls.
  • You have placed 77 tokens in “71-80” bin and 23 tokens in “81-90” bin in Box B. This implies that with 77% probability you assume that your teammate received between 71 and 80 Green balls and with 23% probability you assume that your teammate received between 81 and 90 Green balls.
  • This is just an example to illustrate. The chances you have in mind may be completely different.
