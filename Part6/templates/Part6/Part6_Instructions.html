PART 6 Instructions

Page 1 out of 1

 

You are facing 20 separate decisions numbering 1 through 20.
Each of these decisions is a choice between drawing a ball from “Urn A” or “Urn B”. Urn A and Urn B are unrelated with two urns that you faced in the earlier parts of the experiment.


  • In each of the 20 decisions, Urn A has 50 RED balls and 50 BLUE balls, and pays {{g0}} points if the ball drawn from Urn A is red, and 0 points if it is blue. Since each color has a 1/2 chance of being drawn, this means that drawing from Urn A pays {{g0}} points with a chance of 1/2, and pays 0 points with a chance of 1/2.

  • Urn B, on the other hand, has an unknown number of RED and BLUE balls (with a total of 100 balls). It pays a positive amount if the ball drawn from Urn B is RED, and 0 points if it is BLUE. Since the chance of each color being drawn is unknown, the chance of Urn B paying a positive number of points is unknown as well.

  • The only difference between the 20 decisions is the amount paid when a RED is drawn from Urn B.

You will make one choice in the “Switch” column. This choice indicates that you would like to switch from drawing a ball out of Urn A to drawing out of Urn B. For each decision before your choice, a ball will be drawn from Urn A. For each decision after your choice, including the decision where the choice was made, a ball will be drawn from Urn B.

One of these decisions would be randomly selected to determine your payoffs.
