Now, you are facing 10 separate decisions numbering 1 through 10.
Each of these decisions is a choice between “Option A” or “Option B”.

In each of the 10 decisions, Option A pays either {{f1}} points or {{f2}} points. Option B, on the other hand, pays either {{f3}} points or {{f4}} point. The only difference between the 10 decisions is the chances of earning the higher payoff for each option.

To choose an Option for each decision, you will make one choice in the “Switch” column. This choice indicates that you would like to switch from Option A to Option B. For each decision before your choice, Option A will be used for payment. For each decision after your choice, including the decision where the choice was made, Option B will be used.

One of these decisions would be randomly selected to determine your payoffs.
